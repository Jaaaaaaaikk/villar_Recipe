Adobo


Description:

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish renowned for its rich and flavorful taste profile. Its origins trace back to early Filipino cooking techniques, where meats were preserved in vinegar and salt to withstand the tropical climate. Today, adobo remains a staple in Filipino cuisine, with countless variations across the islands. At its core, adobo consists of meat (typically chicken, pork, or a combination of both) simmered in a marinade of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. The ingredients are simple yet impactful, creating a symphony of savory, tangy, and subtly sweet flavors.

Difficulty:

Easy

Time to Prepare:

Preparation: 10 minutes
Estimated Cooking Time: 40 minutes
Total: 50 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or pork, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves to make the marinade.
  2. Add the meat to the marinade and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for best results.
  3. Transfer the meat and marinade to a pot. Add water and bring to a boil.
  4. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the meat is tender.
  5. Season with salt to taste. Serve hot with rice.
