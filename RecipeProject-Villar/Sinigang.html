Sinigang


Description:

Sinigang is a beloved Filipino sour soup known for its tangy and refreshing flavors. This comforting dish features a tamarind-based broth infused with a variety of meats (such as pork, beef, shrimp, or fish) and an assortment of vegetables, creating a delicious and satisfying meal. Sinigang's distinctive sour taste, combined with the richness of its ingredients, makes it a popular choice for both casual meals and special occasions in Filipino cuisine.

Difficulty:

Easy

Time to Prepare:

Preparation: 15 minutes
Estimated Cooking Time: 45 minutes
Total: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork ribs or pork belly, cut into serving pieces
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet (40g) sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • 1 liter water
  • 2 cups radish (labanos), sliced
  • 2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or spinach leaves
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 pieces green chili peppers (siling haba)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Saut√© garlic until fragrant, then add onions and tomatoes. Cook until softened.
  2. Add pork ribs or pork belly to the pot and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour water into the pot and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and let it simmer until the pork is tender, about 30-40 minutes.
  4. Once the pork is tender, add the sinigang mix and stir until fully dissolved.
  5. Add the radish and string beans to the pot and simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Stir in the kangkong or spinach leaves and green chili peppers. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the sourness of the soup by adding more sinigang mix if desired.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your comforting bowl of Sinigang!
