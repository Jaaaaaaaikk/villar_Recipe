Kare-Kare


Description:

Kare-Kare is a classic Filipino stew known for its rich and creamy peanut sauce. This flavorful dish traditionally features tender oxtail, tripe, and vegetables simmered in a thick and savory sauce made from ground roasted peanuts. Kare-Kare is often served with a side of shrimp paste (bagoong) and enjoyed with steamed rice, offering a delicious combination of textures and flavors that delight the palate.

Difficulty:

Moderate

Time to Prepare:

Preparation: 30 minutes
Estimated Cooking Time: 2 hours
Total: 2 hours 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 kg beef tripe, cleaned and sliced into strips (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons annatto seeds (achuete)
  • 1 cup ground roasted peanuts
  • 4 cups beef or chicken broth
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana blossom (puso ng saging), sliced (optional)
  • 2-3 pieces bok choy or pechay, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shrimp paste (bagoong) for serving

Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Saut√© garlic and onions until softened.
  2. Add the oxtail and beef tripe (if using) to the pot. Cook until lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Add the annatto seeds to the pot and cook for a few minutes to release their color. Remove the seeds and discard.
  4. Stir in the ground peanuts and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour in the beef or chicken broth and bring to a boil. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until the meat is tender.
  6. Once the meat is tender, add the eggplant, string beans, and banana blossom (if using). Simmer for another 5-7 minutes or until the vegetables are cooked through.
  7. Stir in the bok choy or pechay and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side. Enjoy your delicious Kare-Kare!
