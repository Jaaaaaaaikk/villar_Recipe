Mechado


Description:

Mechado is a classic Filipino beef stew with Spanish influences, known for its tender beef chunks simmered in a savory tomato-based sauce. This hearty dish typically features marinated beef cooked with potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers, resulting in a flavorful and comforting meal that's perfect for any occasion. Mechado's robust flavors and tender meat make it a beloved dish in Filipino cuisine, often enjoyed with steamed rice for a satisfying dining experience.

Difficulty:

Intermediate

Time to Prepare:

Preparation: 20 minutes
Estimated Cooking Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
Total: 1 hour 50 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef chuck, cut into large cubes
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for sautéing

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, marinate the beef cubes in soy sauce and Worcestershire sauce for at least 15-20 minutes.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onions, and tomatoes until softened.
  3. Add the marinated beef cubes to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for a few minutes to enhance the flavor.
  5. Pour in the beef broth and add the bay leaf. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook for about 1 hour or until the beef is tender.
  6. Once the beef is tender, add the potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers to the pot. Simmer for another 20-30 minutes or until the vegetables are cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your delicious Mechado!
